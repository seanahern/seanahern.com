Learnings

10/12/1988
The following is a list of lessons, phrases, pieces of advice that held some significant weight for me in my twenties.
  • The answers to most emotional, interpersonal, and relationship challenges can be found inside yourself
  • Child-like fun is a critical part of a healthy adulthood
  • Anxiety is subconscious suppression of fear. Find healthy ways to remove the suppression and express the feelings attached to your fear and you’ll be freed
  • Get extra sleep
  • As early as possible, contribute to retirement savings in a low-fee fund. Automate the contribution, and max it out if you can afford to do so
  • Live within your means, split any surplus between stable investments and doing things that make you happy
  • Make time to travel, or at least time to spend in the great outdoors
  • Your body is your greatest asset. Protect the asset
  • Practice active listening. Respond instead of reacting. Ask questions, and don’t craft a reply while listening
  • Maintaining your health, honing your craft, and building authentic relationships are the key strategies to healthy, manageable career growth. Everything else is a shortcut, or bet, that may or may not yield growth
  • Seek mentorship, and strive to be a mentor to your peers
  • Sharing love (platonic or romantic) is the greatest act of conscious life. Your top 3 favorite books probably include at least one that carries this theme
